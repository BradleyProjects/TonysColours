Abrasives

Spray painting and panel beating are both automotive painting processes that require the use of abrasives. Abrasives are materials that are used to remove paint, rust, or other materials from a surface through the process of sanding, grinding, or polishing. There are a variety of different types of abrasives that can be used in these processes, and the type that is best for a particular job will depend on the surface that needs to be cleaned and the amount of material that needs to be removed.

Mixing Cups

Paint cups are used primarily in the automotive painting industry to achieve the correct mixtures of paint prior to application.

Paint is never applied straight. It is first mixed with an activator solution and occasionally a third component like a paint thinner or reducer.

Since paint manufacturers provide exact ratios for each of these products, the math can get tricky.

﻿That is why most people use mixing cups, which have all of the ratios written on the side, just like a measuring cup.

Silicone Remover

When it comes to removing silicone sealant, sometimes rubbing alcohol alone may not do the trick. A silicone dissolving solvent or silicone remover can be a more effective solution. Before using these products, it's essential to read the instructions carefully and take appropriate safety precautions. To remove the remaining adhesive properties of the silicone, use the silicone remover as directed. Once the silicone has been dissolved, wipe away the residue with a warm, wet cloth. Be careful not to damage the surface underneath, especially if it's painted. Diluting the silicone dissolving solvent or using a mild solution of warm, soapy water can also help to minimize the risk of paint damage.
